My Master's Thesis

Applications of Synthetic Spatiotemporal Signals In Mammalian Cells @ Westmeyer Lab & Simmel Lab in Munich Technical University

MinDE protein oscillations expressed in HEK cells, with unique barcode frequencies. Green sfGFP-MinD, red MinE-mCherry.
This thesis explores whether self-organizing protein patterns inside mammalian cells can be used to move transmembrane proteins and generate synthetic signals at the cell surface. I reconstituted MinDE oscillations in HEK293T cells and developed image analysis tools to quantify their dynamics. My aim was to couple these intracellular waves to an engineered transmembrane protein in order to create a spatially modulated magnetic output signal outside the cell. The broader goal is to enable programmable cell‚Äìenvironment communication using synthetic intracellular dynamics.
